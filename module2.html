Understanding Your Care Options

Knowing where to go for medical care can save you hundreds or even thousands of dollars. Different types of care come with different costs, and choosing the right option for your situation is crucial.

Emergency Room (ER)

üí∞ Typical Cost: $1,500 - $3,000+

Use for life-threatening emergencies only.

When to use:
  • Chest pain or trouble breathing
  • Severe bleeding or head injury
  • Loss of consciousness
  • Stroke symptoms (FAST test)
  • Severe allergic reactions
  • Suspected broken bones

Urgent Care

üí∞ Typical Cost: $100 - $300

For issues that need same-day care but aren't emergencies.

When to use:
  • Minor cuts needing stitches
  • Sprains or minor fractures
  • Fever or flu symptoms
  • Ear or sinus infections
  • UTI symptoms
  • Mild asthma attacks

Primary Care Doctor

üí∞ Typical Cost: $20 - $50 copay

Your first stop for non-urgent health concerns.

When to use:
  • Annual check-ups
  • Chronic condition management
  • Vaccinations
  • Referrals to specialists
  • Preventive care screenings
  • Prescription refills

Telehealth / Virtual Care

üí∞ Typical Cost: $0 - $30

Convenient online video or phone consultations.

When to use:
  • Cold, flu, or allergy symptoms
  • Minor skin conditions
  • Mental health counseling
  • Medication management
  • Follow-up appointments
  • Prescription refills
