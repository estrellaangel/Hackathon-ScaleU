What is Telehealth?

Telehealth (also called telemedicine or virtual care) allows you to consult with healthcare providers using your phone, tablet, or computer. It's convenient, often cheaper than in-person visits, and can address many common health concerns.

How to Access Telehealth

1

Check Your Insurance Coverage

Most plans cover telehealth visits. Check your insurance card or call your insurer to confirm. Many plans now offer $0 copay for telehealth.

2

Choose a Telehealth Provider

You have several options:

  • Your doctor's office: Many primary care offices now offer video visits
  • Insurance telehealth service: Check your insurer's app (e.g., UnitedHealthcare's Virtual Visits)
  • Standalone services: Teladoc, MDLive, Doctor on Demand (check if in-network)
  • School health center: Many universities offer virtual visits for students
3

Download the App or Visit Website

Most telehealth services have mobile apps. Download it, create an account, and add your insurance information.

4

Request a Visit

Describe your symptoms and request a visit. Some services connect you immediately, others schedule appointments.

Tip: Have your insurance card, photo ID, and list of medications ready before your visit.
5

Connect with a Provider

Join the video call when it's time. The provider will ask about your symptoms, review your medical history, and provide recommendations or prescriptions.

üí° Real-World Example: Emma's UTI

Situation: Emma, a college sophomore, starts experiencing UTI symptoms on Sunday evening. Her doctor's office is closed until Monday.

What Emma did:
  1. Checked her UnitedHealthcare app and saw telehealth was covered ($0 copay)
  2. Clicked "Virtual Visit" and described her symptoms
  3. Connected with a doctor via video in 15 minutes
  4. Received a prescription sent to her pharmacy
  5. Picked up medication the same evening
Result: Emma got treatment quickly without leaving her dorm, paid $0 for the visit, and saved herself a trip to urgent care (which would have cost $150+).

üìù Tips for a Successful Telehealth Visit

Find a quiet, well-lit space

Make sure the provider can see and hear you clearly.

Test your technology first

Check camera, microphone, and internet connection before your appointment.

Have information ready

Insurance card, medication list, and symptom details at hand.

Write down questions in advance

Don't forget to ask what you need during the visit.

Take notes

Write down the provider's recommendations and any prescriptions.
